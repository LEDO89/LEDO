# **Policy Governance Model**

## **1\. Purpose**

The Policy Governance Model is a Core Specification that defines policies, permissions, roles, clearances, approval authorities, policy engines, policy conflict resolution, decision-making under communication loss, emergency policy activation, policy exceptions, policy lifecycle, and policy audit mechanisms in an ontology-centric platform.

This document answers the following questions:

Who can do what?  
Under what conditions is an action allowed?  
Under what conditions is an action blocked?  
Which actions require approval, and by whom?  
When policies conflict with each other, what criteria resolve the conflict?  
When communication is lost, how long can the last Evidence or State be used?  
In an emergency, which policy can act before the normal approval flow?  
When an emergency policy is activated, who is responsible and how is it traced?  
Who creates, reviews, approves, and retires policies?  
Which policies govern ActionCandidates generated by an SLM or LLM?

In this platform, policy is not merely a permission check.

Policy is an operational control structure that considers the following elements together:

canonical object  
role  
clearance  
permission  
event type  
action type  
state model  
evidence model  
risk level  
approval level  
policy version  
policy resolution  
network condition  
emergency condition  
audit requirement

The core principles are:

Policy governs permission.  
Decision selects the route.  
Approval grants authority.  
Safety Gate validates execution eligibility.  
Execution Adapter requests external systems.  
Audit preserves accountability.

In plain terms:

Policy defines the conditions for permission.  
Decision selects the route.  
Approval grants authority.  
Safety Gate validates validity and safety immediately before execution.  
Execution Adapter requests external systems.  
Audit preserves responsibility and traceability.

## **5\. Difference Between Policy, Decision, Approval, and Safety Gate**

### **5.1 Policy**

Policy defines allowed and forbidden conditions.

Examples:

HIGH\_RISK action requires Safety Manager approval.  
AI\_DERIVED\_ONLY evidence cannot be used as the sole basis for emergency action.  
Production PLCAdapter cannot be enabled without ExternalSystemOwner approval.

---

### **5.2 Decision**

Decision is the judgment route.

Example:

ZoneRiskState.CRITICAL  
\+ verified gas sensor evidence  
\+ workers present  
→ EMERGENCY\_FAST\_PATH

A Decision determines which route the situation should follow.

---

### **5.3 Approval**

Approval is the actual authority-granting record for a specific situation.

Example:

SafetyManager\_001 approved ACTION\_STOP\_WORK for Zone\_A.

---

### **5.4 Safety Gate**

Safety Gate is the validation boundary immediately before execution.

Examples:

Approval still valid?  
Evidence still fresh?  
State still safe?  
No TOCTOU delta?  
Adapter available?  
Network healthy?  
Audit trace prepared?

Even if an action is allowed by policy and has been approved, an ExecutionRequest must not be created unless it passes the Safety Gate.

---

## **6\. PAP / PDP / PEP / PIP**

The policy system is divided into four core components.

### **6.1 PAP: Policy Administration Point**

The PAP is the point where policies are authored and managed.

Responsibilities:

policy creation  
policy modification  
policy review  
policy approval  
policy deployment  
policy retirement  
policy version management  
policy change audit

Examples:

policy\_registry/  
approval\_rules.yaml  
emergency\_policies.yaml  
rego\_policy\_bundle/

---

### **6.2 PDP: Policy Decision Point**

The PDP performs policy evaluation.

Responsibilities:

receive policy context  
evaluate policy rules  
return results such as ALLOW / DENY / REQUIRE\_APPROVAL

Examples:

DummyPDP  
LocalPolicyEngine  
OPA / Rego  
Enterprise PDP

The PDP does not perform physical execution.

The PDP only returns policy decision results.

---

### **6.3 PEP: Policy Enforcement Point**

The PEP enforces PDP results at actual system boundaries.

Examples:

API Gateway  
Decision Router  
Safety Gate  
Execution Adapter  
External Adapter Boundary

---

### **6.4 PIP: Policy Information Point**

The PIP provides the information required by the PDP for policy evaluation.

Examples:

World State Cache  
Ontology Store  
Evidence Store  
Role Registry  
Approval Registry  
Policy Registry  
User Directory  
Device Health Snapshot  
Network Health Snapshot

## **7\. Policy Decision Result**

A policy decision result must not be a simple true/false value.

Recommended result values:

ALLOW  
DENY  
REQUIRE\_APPROVAL  
REQUIRE\_EVIDENCE  
REQUIRE\_REVALIDATION  
REQUIRE\_FAIL\_SAFE  
REQUIRE\_MANUAL\_OVERRIDE  
REQUIRE\_POLICY\_EXCEPTION\_REVIEW

Meanings:

`ALLOW` means the policy allows the action.  
`DENY` means the policy forbids the action.  
`REQUIRE_APPROVAL` means additional approval is required.  
`REQUIRE_EVIDENCE` means evidence is insufficient.  
`REQUIRE_REVALIDATION` means revalidation is required.  
`REQUIRE_FAIL_SAFE` means a fail-safe route is required.  
`REQUIRE_MANUAL_OVERRIDE` means human manual intervention is required.  
`REQUIRE_POLICY_EXCEPTION_REVIEW` means a policy exception review is required.

## **8\. Policy Type**

Policy types are classified as follows:

Access Policy  
Approval Policy

Adapter Policy  
Data Privacy Policy  
Audit Policy

---

### **8.1 Access Policy**

Access Policy defines which users can access which objects or functions.

Examples:

Only supervisors can view high-risk zone details.  
Only auditors can export audit logs.

---

### **8.2 Approval Policy**

Approval Policy defines which approval level is required for each action.

Examples:

ACTION\_STOP\_WORK requires SAFETY\_MANAGER\_APPROVAL.  
ACTION\_SEND\_DASHBOARD\_ALERT requires NO\_APPROVAL.

---

### **8.3 Safety Policy**

Safety Policy defines safety-related decision criteria.

Examples:

High-risk action cannot proceed with stale evidence.  
Unresolved evidence conflict requires escalation.

---

### **8.4 Emergency Policy**

Emergency Policy defines the conditions for the emergency fast-path.

Example:

Emergency evacuation may bypass normal human approval only if:  
\- emergency policy is active  
\- minimum verified evidence exists  
\- target is valid  
\- Safety Gate is required  
\- post-hoc audit is required

---

### **8.5 Evidence Policy**

Evidence Policy defines the trustworthiness and usability of evidence.

Evidence Policy must directly reference fields from `05_evidence_model/`.

Representative fields:

source\_trust\_level  
time\_trust\_level  
clock\_sync\_status  
clock\_drift\_estimate\_ms  
device\_health\_snapshot\_ref  
device\_health\_status  
calibration\_status  
freshness\_status  
trust\_upgrade\_status  
attestation\_status  
conflict\_status  
privacy\_lifecycle\_status

Examples:

AI\_DERIVED\_ONLY evidence cannot be used as the sole basis for high-risk approval.  
CRITICAL\_EMERGENCY requires VERIFIED\_DEVICE or TRUSTED\_SYSTEM evidence.  
Evidence with CLOCK\_DRIFT\_EXCEEDED cannot support emergency action.  
Evidence from degraded device requires manual review or fail-safe route.

The `ATTESTED_AI_DERIVED` state must be connected to the `trust_upgrade_status` in the Evidence Model.

Example:

AI\_DERIVED\_ONLY  
→ policy support only  
→ cannot approve  
→ cannot execute

ATTESTED\_AI\_DERIVED  
→ may support interpretation  
→ requires trust\_upgrade\_status \= VERIFIED\_BY\_RULE or VERIFIED\_BY\_HUMAN or VERIFIED\_BY\_SYSTEM  
→ cannot be the sole basis for emergency bypass

In other words, AI-generated summaries or interpretations may serve as supporting evidence, but they must not be the sole basis for high-risk approval or emergency execution.

---

### **8.6 State Freshness Policy**

State Freshness Policy defines how fresh state data must be.

Examples:

WorkerLocationState must be fresh enough for high-risk zone actions.  
RobotPoseState must be fresh enough for robot mission actions.

Actual TTL values must be defined by the domain expert.

Codex must not invent them.

State Freshness Policy must reference the following fields:

state\_updated\_at  
state\_freshness\_status  
state\_freshness\_ttl  
last\_verified\_state\_snapshot\_ref  
state\_conflict\_status  
state\_reconciliation\_status

---

### **8.7 Execution Policy**

Execution Policy defines the conditions under which an ExecutionRequest can be created.

Examples:

Physical execution is disabled in development environment.  
Only DryRunAdapter or MockAdapter is allowed in MVP.

---

### **8.8 Adapter Policy**

Adapter Policy defines the conditions for using external system adapters.

Examples:

PLCAdapter production mode requires explicit approval.  
SCADA write adapter must not be enabled by default.

---

### **8.9 AI Governance Policy**

AI Governance Policy defines the allowed scope of AI outputs.

Allowed AI roles:

Intent  
ActionCandidate  
EvidenceSummary  
MappingProposal  
RiskInterpretation  
Explanation

PolicyImpactSuggestion

Forbidden AI roles:

ApprovedAction  
EmergencyApprovedAction  
ExecutionRequest  
ExternalControlRequest  
PLC command  
robot command  
SCADA command  
emergency bypass decision  
physical execution decision

Core principle:

AI may propose.  
AI must not approve.  
AI must not execute.

### **8.10 SLM ActionCandidate Governance**

The same policy boundaries apply when a commercial or local SLM generates an ActionCandidate.

For example, a Qwen-based SLM or a field-specific small model may generate:

However, an SLM must not generate:

ApprovedAction  
EmergencyApprovedAction  
ExecutionRequest  
ExternalControlRequest

An ActionCandidate generated by an SLM must pass through the following route:

SLM Output  
→ ActionCandidate  
→ Policy Governance Check  
→ Decision / Approval Matrix

→ Safety Gate  
→ ExecutionRequest

SLM output must include the following governance metadata:

trace\_id  
decision\_trace\_id

---

### **8.11 Ontology Governance Policy**

Ontology Governance Policy controls ontology changes and module boundaries.

Examples:

Core ontology changes require Ontology Steward approval.  
Domain module cannot directly define cross-domain object properties without mediation.

A Domain module must not arbitrarily override Core policies.

In other words:

Domain-specific policy may extend core policy.  
Domain-specific policy must not weaken core safety policy.  
Domain-specific policy must not bypass core audit policy.  
Domain-specific policy must not override emergency policy preconditions.

If a domain policy proposes weaker conditions than a Core policy, the Policy Resolution Strategy must prioritize the Core Safety Policy.

## **11\. Clearance Model**

Clearance means qualification, certification, or risk-level access authority required to perform or approve a specific task.

Role and Clearance are different.

Role  
→ organizational position or responsibility

Clearance  
→ qualification level for accessing a specific risky task, system, zone, equipment, or policy

Examples:

Supervisor is a Role.  
CLEARANCE\_CONFINED\_SPACE is a Clearance.

SafetyManager is a Role.  
CLEARANCE\_ROBOT\_OPERATION is a separate Clearance.

ExternalSystemOwner is a Role.  
CLEARANCE\_PRODUCTION\_ADAPTER\_ENABLE is a separate Clearance.

Recommended Clearance values:

CLEARANCE\_NONE

CLEARANCE\_SAFETY\_LEVEL\_2  
CLEARANCE\_ROBOT\_OPERATION  
CLEARANCE\_ELECTRICAL\_WORK

CLEARANCE\_EMERGENCY\_RESPONSE  
CLEARANCE\_POLICY\_ADMIN  
CLEARANCE\_ONTOLOGY\_ADMIN  
CLEARANCE\_PRODUCTION\_ADAPTER\_ENABLE

Important rules:

Role alone is not enough for high-risk authority.  
Clearance alone is not enough without the required role.  
High-risk approval requires both Role and Clearance.

For example, a Supervisor may not have authority to approve confined space work.

A SafetyManager may not have authority to enable a robot production adapter.

## **14\. Policy Resolution Strategy**

Policy conflict resolution rules are a core part of the Policy Governance Model.

Multiple policies may apply to the same action at the same time.

For example, one action may be governed by:

Access Policy  
Approval Policy  
Safety Policy  
Evidence Policy  
Execution Policy  
Emergency Policy  
Adapter Policy  
AI Governance Policy

These policies may conflict with one another.

---

### **14.1 Types of Policy Conflicts**

Representative conflict types:

ALLOW vs DENY conflict  
low-risk route vs high-risk evidence requirement conflict  
normal approval policy vs emergency policy conflict  
global policy vs domain policy conflict  
old policy version vs new policy version conflict  
role permission vs clearance requirement conflict  
execution policy vs adapter policy conflict  
AI governance policy vs action candidate policy conflict

Example:

Approval Policy:  
Supervisor can approve ACTION\_LOCK\_ZONE.

Safety Policy:  
ACTION\_LOCK\_ZONE in HIGH\_RISK zone requires SafetyManager approval.

Resolution:  
Safety Policy wins.

---

### **14.2 Policy Conflict Resolution Principles**

Policy conflicts are resolved according to the following priority principles:

1\. Safety-first policy wins.  
2\. DENY beats ALLOW for high-risk or uncertain cases.  
3\. More specific policy beats general policy.  
4\. Stricter policy beats permissive policy.  
5\. Emergency policy may override normal policy only if preconditions are satisfied.  
6\. Active latest policy version beats older active-compatible versions.  
7\. Policy exception must be explicit, scoped, time-limited, and audited.  
8\. Unresolved conflict blocks high-risk execution.

---

### **14.3 Policy Priority Matrix**

The default priority order for policy conflicts is:

1\. Emergency Policy  
2\. Safety Policy  
3\. Evidence Policy  
4\. State Freshness Policy  
5\. Execution Policy  
6\. Adapter Policy  
7\. AI Governance Policy  
8\. Ontology Governance Policy  
9\. Approval Policy  
10\. Access Policy  
11\. Data Privacy Policy  
12\. Audit Policy

However, this is not a simple strength ranking.

Even though Emergency Policy is listed first, it cannot operate unless its emergency preconditions are satisfied.

Audit Policy may appear lower in the list, but it is a cross-cutting policy that must attach to all safety-related policy outcomes.

Data Privacy Policy may not function primarily as a safety execution blocker, but it must be enforced for access to and storage of sensitive information.

Examples of policy conflict resolution:

Safety Policy vs Access Policy  
→ Safety Policy wins.

Evidence Policy vs AI Governance Policy  
→ Evidence Policy wins, but AI Governance Policy still records model restrictions.

Emergency Policy vs Normal Approval Policy  
→ Emergency Policy may win only if emergency preconditions are satisfied.

Execution Policy vs Adapter Policy  
→ Stricter rule wins. If either blocks production execution, execution is blocked.

Domain Policy vs Core Safety Policy  
→ Core Safety Policy wins.

---

### **14.4 Policy Conflict Resolution Results**

Recommended result values:

RESOLVED\_BY\_SAFETY\_PRIORITY  
RESOLVED\_BY\_SPECIFICITY

## **15\. Grace Period for Evidence / State**

When communication is lost, it becomes difficult to determine the freshness of Evidence and State.

For example, if communication between the Edge Gateway and the central server is lost, the central system may no longer receive the latest World State.

If every action is stopped immediately, field operations may be paralyzed.

However, if stale state is trusted indefinitely, safety incidents may occur.

Therefore, a Grace Period is required.

---

### **15.1 Definition of Grace Period**

A Grace Period is a policy-controlled time window that allows the last verified Evidence or State to be used only for a limited period and only for limited purposes during communication loss.

A Grace Period is not unconditional permission.

A Grace Period is a temporary allowance window during communication loss.  
A Grace Period is not a mechanism for trusting stale data indefinitely.  
A Grace Period is not an exception that freely permits risky actions.

---

### **15.2 Grace Period Evaluation Conditions**

A Grace Period must reference time trust, device health, and source trust information from the Evidence Model.

Evaluation conditions:

connectivity\_status  
disconnected\_since  
last\_successful\_sync\_at  
last\_verified\_state\_snapshot\_ref  
last\_verified\_evidence\_bundle\_ref  
state\_freshness\_ttl  
evidence\_freshness\_ttl  
source\_trust\_level  
time\_trust\_level  
clock\_sync\_status  
clock\_drift\_estimate\_ms  
device\_health\_snapshot\_ref  
device\_health\_status  
calibration\_status  
trust\_upgrade\_status  
risk\_level  
action\_type  
offline\_policy\_ref  
local\_edge\_policy\_available

---

### **15.3 Grace Period Result Values**

Recommended result values:

GRACE\_NOT\_REQUIRED  
GRACE\_ALLOWED\_MONITORING\_ONLY  
GRACE\_ALLOWED\_LOCAL\_SAFE\_ACTION\_ONLY  
GRACE\_ALLOWED\_FAIL\_SAFE\_ONLY  
GRACE\_DENIED  
FAIL\_SAFE\_REQUIRED  
MANUAL\_OVERRIDE\_REQUIRED

---

### **15.4 Grace Period Allowance Principles**

During communication loss, what is allowed must be limited.

Examples of allowed actions:

local alarm  
local dashboard warning  
safe stop  
return to safe zone  
hold task  
request manual inspection  
audit-only record

Examples of forbidden or restricted actions:

new high-risk approval based only on stale state  
production PLC write  
SCADA write  
robot mission expansion  
zone unlock  
permit override  
policy exception approval

---

### **15.5 Core Grace Period Principles**

Stale state may support local safe action.  
Stale state must not support new risky execution.  
Communication loss must reduce authority, not expand it.

In plain terms:

Stale state may serve as the basis for local safe actions.  
Stale state must not serve as the basis for new risky execution.  
Communication loss is a condition that reduces authority, not one that expands it.

---

### **15.6 Grace Period Scenario 1: Gas Critical \+ Communication Loss**

Situation:

GasSensor\_01 detects CRITICAL gas level.  
Edge Gateway loses connection to central server.  
Last verified gas sensor evidence is recent within local grace period.  
Device health status is OK.  
Clock sync status was valid before disconnection.  
Workers may be present in the zone.

Allowed policy results:

GRACE\_ALLOWED\_LOCAL\_SAFE\_ACTION\_ONLY  
or  
GRACE\_ALLOWED\_FAIL\_SAFE\_ONLY

Allowed actions:

local alarm  
local evacuation warning  
local safe stop  
work hold  
post-reconnect audit

Actions that must not be allowed:

new remote high-risk approval  
production PLC write without local fail-safe authority  
zone unlock  
permit override

Core judgment:

Communication loss does not expand authority.  
However, if verified danger evidence already exists and a local fail-safe policy is available,  
local safe actions may be allowed.

---

### **15.7 Grace Period Scenario 2: Robot Mission \+ Stale Pose State**

Situation:

Network heartbeat is degraded.

## **16\. Emergency Policy Bypass**

Emergency Policy Bypass is a pre-approved policy that can operate before the normal approval flow in an emergency.

The important point is:

Emergency Policy Bypass is not no-approval.  
Emergency Policy Bypass is a pre-approved emergency policy.  
Emergency Policy Bypass requires post-hoc audit.

---

### **16.1 Why Emergency Policy Bypass Is Needed**

In an emergency, waiting for human approval may cause greater harm.

Examples:

gas critical threshold exceeded  
worker entered danger zone  
robot moving toward human  
fire detected  
structural collapse risk detected  
confined space emergency

In these cases, actions such as local alarm, evacuation request, stop work, or safe stop may be required quickly under a pre-approved emergency policy.

---

### **16.2 Emergency Policy Bypass Activation Conditions**

Emergency Policy Bypass must satisfy the following conditions:

emergency\_policy\_status \= ACTIVE  
risk\_level \= CRITICAL\_EMERGENCY  
minimum\_verified\_evidence\_exists \= true

## **34\. Final Principles**

The Policy Governance Model is the policy constitution of the platform.

However, policy does not execute by itself.

Policy defines permission conditions.  
Policy Resolution resolves policy conflicts.  
Grace Period limits authority under communication loss.  
Emergency Policy Bypass is a pre-approved emergency policy.  
Decision selects the judgment route.  
Approval grants authority.  
Safety Gate validates immediately before execution.  
Execution Adapter requests external systems.  
Audit records responsibility.

Policies must be applied based on canonical objects.

Policies must reference the Evidence Model fields for source trust, time trust, clock sync, device health, and trust upgrade status.

Policies must be versioned.

Policy conflicts must be resolved according to the safety-first principle.

Communication loss is not a condition that expands authority; it is a condition that limits authority.

Emergency Policy Bypass is not no-approval. It is a pre-approved emergency policy.

Emergency Policy Bypass must require post-hoc audit.

Policy Exception must include a validity period, scope, compensating controls, and audit record.

Role is an organizational position, and Clearance is a qualification. High-risk authority requires both.

SLMs and LLMs may propose ActionCandidates, but they must not approve or execute.

The final rule is:

Policy governs permission.  
Resolution handles conflict.  
Grace Period limits authority under disconnection.  
Emergency Bypass requires pre-approved policy and post-hoc audit.  
Safety Gate protects execution.  
Audit preserves accountability.

# **Policy Governance Model**

# **1\. 목적**

Policy Governance Model은 온톨로지 중심 플랫폼에서 **정책, 권한, 역할, 자격, 승인 권한, 정책 엔진, 정책 충돌 해결, 통신 단절 시 판단, 비상 정책 활성화, 정책 예외, 정책 생명주기, 정책 감사 체계**를 정의하는 Core Specification이다.

이 문서는 다음 질문에 답한다.

누가 무엇을 할 수 있는가?  
어떤 조건에서 허용되는가?  
어떤 조건에서 차단되는가?  
어떤 행동은 누구의 승인이 필요한가?  
정책끼리 충돌하면 어떤 기준으로 해결하는가?  
통신이 끊겼을 때 마지막 Evidence 또는 State를 얼마 동안 사용할 수 있는가?  
비상 상황에서 어떤 정책이 일반 승인 흐름보다 먼저 작동할 수 있는가?  
비상 정책이 작동했을 때 누가 책임지고 어떻게 추적하는가?  
정책은 누가 만들고, 검토하고, 승인하고, 폐기하는가?  
SLM 또는 LLM이 생성한 ActionCandidate는 어떤 정책으로 통제되는가?

이 플랫폼에서 정책은 단순한 권한 체크가 아니다.

정책은 다음 요소를 함께 고려하는 **운영 통제 구조**다.

canonical object  
role  
clearance  
permission  
event type  
action type  
state model  
evidence model  
risk level  
approval level  
policy version  
policy resolution  
network condition  
emergency condition  
audit requirement

핵심 원칙은 다음과 같다.

Policy governs permission.  
Decision selects the route.  
Approval grants authority.  
Safety Gate validates execution eligibility.  
Execution Adapter requests external systems.  
Audit preserves accountability.

한글로 정리하면 다음과 같다.

정책은 허용 조건을 정의한다.  
판단은 경로를 선택한다.  
승인은 권한을 부여한다.  
Safety Gate는 실행 직전 유효성과 안전성을 검증한다.  
Execution Adapter는 외부 시스템에 요청한다.  
Audit은 책임과 추적성을 보존한다.

# **2\. Core Specification 안에서의 위치**

Policy Governance Model은 Core Specifications의 08번 문서다.

03\_core\_specifications/  
  00\_canonical\_object\_lifecycle/  
  01\_common\_schema\_dto/  
  02\_event\_type\_taxonomy/  
  03\_action\_type\_registry/  
  04\_state\_model\_registry/  
  05\_evidence\_model/  
  06\_ontology\_module\_boundary/  
  07\_decision\_approval\_matrix/  
  08\_policy\_governance\_model/  
  09\_execution\_adapter\_model/  
  10\_audit\_observability\_model/

이 순서는 중요하다.

`Decision / Approval Matrix`는 어떤 판단 경로와 승인 수준이 필요한지를 정의한다.

`Policy Governance Model`은 그 판단 경로와 승인 수준이 **어떤 정책, 역할, 자격, 조건, 책임 구조에 의해 지배되는지** 정의한다.

즉:

Decision / Approval Matrix  
→ “이 상황은 Safety Manager Approval이 필요하다.”

Policy Governance Model  
→ “Safety Manager가 누구이며, 어떤 Clearance가 필요하고,

   어떤 감사 기록을 남겨야 하는가.”

# **5\. Policy, Decision, Approval, Safety Gate의 차이**

## **5.1 Policy**

Policy는 허용 조건과 금지 조건이다.

예시:

HIGH\_RISK action requires Safety Manager approval.  
AI\_DERIVED\_ONLY evidence cannot be used as the sole basis for emergency action.  
Production PLCAdapter cannot be enabled without ExternalSystemOwner approval.

---

## **5.2 Decision**

Decision은 판단 경로다.

예시:

ZoneRiskState.CRITICAL  
\+ verified gas sensor evidence  
\+ workers present  
→ EMERGENCY\_FAST\_PATH

Decision은 “이 상황이 어떤 경로로 가야 하는가”를 판단한다.

---

## **5.3 Approval**

Approval은 특정 상황에 대한 실제 권한 부여 기록이다.

예시:

SafetyManager\_001 approved ACTION\_STOP\_WORK for Zone\_A.

---

## **5.4 Safety Gate**

Safety Gate는 실행 직전 검증이다.

예시:

Approval still valid?  
Evidence still fresh?  
State still safe?  
No TOCTOU delta?  
Adapter available?  
Network healthy?  
Audit trace prepared?

정책상 허용되고 승인까지 되었더라도 Safety Gate를 통과하지 못하면 ExecutionRequest는 생성되면 안 된다.

---

# **6\. PAP / PDP / PEP / PIP**

정책 시스템은 네 가지 핵심 구성 요소로 나눈다.

## **6.1 PAP: Policy Administration Point**

PAP는 정책을 작성하고 관리하는 지점이다.

역할:

정책 작성  
정책 수정  
정책 검토  
정책 승인  
정책 배포  
정책 폐기  
정책 버전 관리  
정책 변경 감사

예시:

policy\_registry/  
approval\_rules.yaml  
emergency\_policies.yaml  
rego\_policy\_bundle/

---

## **6.2 PDP: Policy Decision Point**

PDP는 정책 판단을 수행하는 지점이다.

역할:

정책 context를 받는다.  
정책 규칙을 평가한다.  
ALLOW / DENY / REQUIRE\_APPROVAL 같은 결과를 반환한다.

예시:

DummyPDP  
LocalPolicyEngine  
OPA / Rego  
Enterprise PDP

PDP는 물리 실행을 하지 않는다.  
PDP는 정책 판단 결과만 반환한다.

---

## **6.3 PEP: Policy Enforcement Point**

PEP는 PDP 판단 결과를 실제 시스템 경계에서 강제하는 지점이다.

예시:

API Gateway  
Decision Router  
Safety Gate  
Execution Adapter  
External Adapter Boundary

---

## **6.4 PIP: Policy Information Point**

PIP는 PDP가 판단할 때 필요한 정보를 제공하는 지점이다.

예시:

World State Cache  
Ontology Store  
Evidence Store  
Role Registry  
Approval Registry  
Policy Registry  
User Directory  
Device Health Snapshot  
Network Health Snapshot

---

# **7\. Policy Decision Result**

정책 판단 결과는 단순 true/false가 아니어야 한다.

추천 결과값은 다음과 같다.

ALLOW  
DENY  
REQUIRE\_APPROVAL  
REQUIRE\_EVIDENCE  
REQUIRE\_REVALIDATION  
REQUIRE\_FAIL\_SAFE  
REQUIRE\_MANUAL\_OVERRIDE  
REQUIRE\_POLICY\_EXCEPTION\_REVIEW

각 의미는 다음과 같다.

`ALLOW`는 정책상 허용을 의미한다.  
`DENY`는 정책상 금지를 의미한다.  
`REQUIRE_APPROVAL`은 추가 승인이 필요함을 의미한다.  
`REQUIRE_EVIDENCE`는 증거가 부족함을 의미한다.  
`REQUIRE_REVALIDATION`은 재검증이 필요함을 의미한다.  
`REQUIRE_FAIL_SAFE`는 fail-safe 경로가 필요함을 의미한다.  
`REQUIRE_MANUAL_OVERRIDE`는 사람의 수동 개입이 필요함을 의미한다.  
`REQUIRE_POLICY_EXCEPTION_REVIEW`는 정책 예외 검토가 필요함을 의미한다.

# **8\. Policy Type**

정책 유형은 다음과 같이 나눈다.

Safety Policy

Evidence Policy  
State Freshness Policy  
Execution Policy  
Adapter Policy  
Data Privacy Policy  
Audit Policy  
Ontology Governance Policy  
AI Governance Policy

---

## **8.1 Access Policy**

사용자가 어떤 객체나 기능에 접근할 수 있는지 정의한다.

예시:

Only supervisors can view high-risk zone details.  
Only auditors can export audit logs.

---

## **8.2 Approval Policy**

어떤 행동에 어떤 승인 수준이 필요한지 정의한다.

예시:

ACTION\_STOP\_WORK requires SAFETY\_MANAGER\_APPROVAL.  
ACTION\_SEND\_DASHBOARD\_ALERT requires NO\_APPROVAL.

---

## **8.3 Safety Policy**

안전 관련 판단 기준을 정의한다.

예시:

High-risk action cannot proceed with stale evidence.  
Unresolved evidence conflict requires escalation.

---

## **8.4 Emergency Policy**

Emergency fast-path 조건을 정의한다.

예시:

Emergency evacuation may bypass normal human approval only if:  
\- emergency policy is active  
\- minimum verified evidence exists  
\- target is valid  
\- Safety Gate is required  
\- post-hoc audit is required

---

## **8.5 Evidence Policy**

Evidence Policy는 증거의 신뢰도와 사용 가능성을 정의한다.

Evidence Policy는 반드시 `05_evidence_model/`의 필드를 직접 참조해야 한다.

참조해야 하는 대표 필드는 다음과 같다.

source\_trust\_level  
time\_trust\_level  
clock\_sync\_status  
clock\_drift\_estimate\_ms  
device\_health\_snapshot\_ref  
device\_health\_status  
calibration\_status  
freshness\_status  
trust\_upgrade\_status  
attestation\_status  
conflict\_status  
privacy\_lifecycle\_status

예시:

AI\_DERIVED\_ONLY evidence cannot be used as the sole basis for high-risk approval.  
CRITICAL\_EMERGENCY requires VERIFIED\_DEVICE or TRUSTED\_SYSTEM evidence.  
Evidence with CLOCK\_DRIFT\_EXCEEDED cannot support emergency action.  
Evidence from degraded device requires manual review or fail-safe route.

`ATTESTED_AI_DERIVED` 상태는 Evidence Model의 `trust_upgrade_status`와 연결되어야 한다.

예시:

AI\_DERIVED\_ONLY  
→ policy support only  
→ cannot approve  
→ cannot execute

ATTESTED\_AI\_DERIVED  
→ may support interpretation  
→ requires trust\_upgrade\_status \= VERIFIED\_BY\_RULE or VERIFIED\_BY\_HUMAN or VERIFIED\_BY\_SYSTEM  
→ cannot be the sole basis for emergency bypass

즉, AI가 생성한 요약이나 판단은 보조 근거가 될 수 있지만, 고위험 승인이나 비상 실행의 단독 근거가 되어서는 안 된다.

---

## **8.6 State Freshness Policy**

상태 데이터가 얼마나 최신이어야 하는지 정의한다.

예시:

WorkerLocationState must be fresh enough for high-risk zone actions.  
RobotPoseState must be fresh enough for robot mission actions.

실제 TTL 값은 도메인 전문가가 정의해야 한다.  
Codex가 임의로 만들면 안 된다.

State Freshness Policy는 다음 필드를 참조해야 한다.

state\_updated\_at  
state\_freshness\_status  
state\_freshness\_ttl  
last\_verified\_state\_snapshot\_ref  
state\_conflict\_status  
state\_reconciliation\_status

---

## **8.7 Execution Policy**

어떤 조건에서 ExecutionRequest를 만들 수 있는지 정의한다.

예시:

Physical execution is disabled in development environment.  
Only DryRunAdapter or MockAdapter is allowed in MVP.

---

## **8.8 Adapter Policy**

외부 시스템 어댑터 사용 조건을 정의한다.

예시:

PLCAdapter production mode requires explicit approval.  
SCADA write adapter must not be enabled by default.

---

## **8.9 AI Governance Policy**

AI 출력의 허용 범위를 정의한다.

허용되는 AI 역할:

MappingProposal

금지되는 AI 역할:

핵심 원칙:

AI는 제안할 수 있다.  
AI는 승인할 수 없다.  
AI는 실행할 수 없다.

---

## **8.10 SLM ActionCandidate Governance**

상업용 또는 로컬 SLM이 ActionCandidate를 생성하는 경우에도 동일한 정책 경계를 적용한다.

예를 들어 Qwen 계열 SLM 또는 현장 특화 소형 모델이 다음을 생성할 수 있다.

action candidate

하지만 SLM은 다음을 생성할 수 없다.

ExecutionRequest  
ExternalControlRequest  
physical command

→ Policy Governance Check  
→ Decision / Approval Matrix

→ Safety Gate

evidence\_refs\_used  
ai\_governance\_policy\_ref  
trace\_id  
decision\_trace\_id

---

## **8.11 Ontology Governance Policy**

온톨로지 변경과 모듈 경계를 통제한다.

예시:

Core ontology changes require Ontology Steward approval.  
Domain module cannot directly define cross-domain object properties without mediation.

Domain module은 Core 정책을 임의로 override할 수 없다.

즉:

Domain-specific policy may extend core policy.  
Domain-specific policy must not weaken core safety policy.  
Domain-specific policy must not bypass core audit policy.  
Domain-specific policy must not override emergency policy preconditions.

도메인 정책이 Core 정책보다 완화된 조건을 제안하는 경우, Policy Resolution Strategy는 Core Safety Policy를 우선해야 한다.

# **11\. Clearance Model**

Clearance는 특정 작업을 수행하거나 승인할 수 있는 자격, 인증, 위험 수준 접근 권한을 의미한다.

Role과 Clearance는 다르다.

Role  
→ 조직 내 직책 또는 책임

Clearance  
→ 특정 위험 작업, 시스템, 구역, 장비, 정책에 접근할 수 있는 자격 수준

예시:

Supervisor는 Role이다.  
CLEARANCE\_CONFINED\_SPACE는 Clearance다.

SafetyManager는 Role이다.  
CLEARANCE\_ROBOT\_OPERATION은 별도 Clearance다.

ExternalSystemOwner는 Role이다.  
CLEARANCE\_PRODUCTION\_ADAPTER\_ENABLE은 별도 Clearance다.

추천 Clearance는 다음과 같다.

CLEARANCE\_NONE  
CLEARANCE\_SITE\_BASIC  
CLEARANCE\_SAFETY\_LEVEL\_1  
CLEARANCE\_SAFETY\_LEVEL\_2  
CLEARANCE\_ROBOT\_OPERATION  
CLEARANCE\_ELECTRICAL\_WORK  
CLEARANCE\_CONFINED\_SPACE  
CLEARANCE\_EMERGENCY\_RESPONSE  
CLEARANCE\_POLICY\_ADMIN  
CLEARANCE\_ONTOLOGY\_ADMIN  
CLEARANCE\_PRODUCTION\_ADAPTER\_ENABLE

중요한 점은 다음과 같다.

Role alone is not enough for high-risk authority.  
Clearance alone is not enough without the required role.  
High-risk approval requires both Role and Clearance.

즉, Supervisor라도 confined space 작업 승인 권한이 없을 수 있다.  
SafetyManager라도 robot production adapter를 enable할 권한은 없을 수 있다.

# **14\. Policy Resolution Strategy**

정책 충돌 해결 규칙은 Policy Governance Model의 핵심이다.

정책은 여러 곳에서 동시에 적용될 수 있다.

예를 들어 하나의 행동에 대해 다음 정책이 동시에 걸릴 수 있다.

Access Policy  
Approval Policy  
Safety Policy  
Evidence Policy  
Execution Policy  
Emergency Policy  
Adapter Policy  
AI Governance Policy

이때 정책끼리 충돌할 수 있다.

---

## **14.1 정책 충돌의 유형**

대표적인 충돌 유형은 다음과 같다.

ALLOW vs DENY conflict  
low-risk route vs high-risk evidence requirement conflict  
normal approval policy vs emergency policy conflict  
global policy vs domain policy conflict  
old policy version vs new policy version conflict  
role permission vs clearance requirement conflict  
execution policy vs adapter policy conflict  
AI governance policy vs action candidate policy conflict

예시:

Approval Policy:  
Supervisor can approve ACTION\_LOCK\_ZONE.

Safety Policy:  
ACTION\_LOCK\_ZONE in HIGH\_RISK zone requires SafetyManager approval.

Resolution:  
Safety Policy wins.

---

## **14.2 정책 충돌 해결 원칙**

정책 충돌은 다음 우선순위로 해결한다.

1\. Safety-first policy wins.  
2\. DENY beats ALLOW for high-risk or uncertain cases.  
3\. More specific policy beats general policy.  
4\. Stricter policy beats permissive policy.  
5\. Emergency policy may override normal policy only if preconditions are satisfied.  
6\. Active latest policy version beats older active-compatible versions.  
7\. Policy exception must be explicit, scoped, time-limited, and audited.  
8\. Unresolved conflict blocks high-risk execution.

---

## **14.3 정책 우선순위 매트릭스**

정책 충돌 시 기본 우선순위는 다음과 같다.

1\. Emergency Policy  
2\. Safety Policy  
3\. Evidence Policy  
4\. State Freshness Policy  
5\. Execution Policy  
6\. Adapter Policy  
7\. AI Governance Policy  
8\. Ontology Governance Policy  
9\. Approval Policy  
10\. Access Policy  
11\. Data Privacy Policy  
12\. Audit Policy

단, 이 순위는 단순한 강약 순서가 아니다.

`Emergency Policy`가 가장 위에 있더라도 비상 정책의 사전 조건이 충족되지 않으면 작동할 수 없다.  
`Audit Policy`는 우선순위가 낮아 보일 수 있지만 모든 안전 관련 정책 결과에 반드시 따라붙어야 하는 횡단 정책이다.  
`Data Privacy Policy`도 안전 실행을 막는 용도와는 다르지만, 민감 정보 접근과 저장에는 강제되어야 한다.

정책 충돌 예시는 다음과 같다.

Safety Policy vs Access Policy  
→ Safety Policy wins.

Evidence Policy vs AI Governance Policy  
→ Evidence Policy wins, but AI Governance Policy still records model restrictions.

Emergency Policy vs Normal Approval Policy  
→ Emergency Policy may win only if emergency preconditions are satisfied.

Execution Policy vs Adapter Policy  
→ Stricter rule wins. If either blocks production execution, execution is blocked.

Domain Policy vs Core Safety Policy  
→ Core Safety Policy wins.

---

## **14.4 정책 충돌 해결 결과**

추천 결과값은 다음과 같다.

NO\_CONFLICT

# **15\. Grace Period for Evidence / State**

통신 단절 상황에서는 Evidence와 State의 최신성을 판단하기 어렵다.

예를 들어 Edge Gateway와 중앙 서버 간 통신이 끊기면, 중앙 시스템은 최신 World State를 받지 못할 수 있다.

하지만 모든 행동을 즉시 중단하면 현장 운영이 마비될 수 있고, 반대로 오래된 상태를 계속 믿으면 안전 사고가 발생할 수 있다.

따라서 Grace Period가 필요하다.

---

## **15.1 Grace Period의 정의**

Grace Period는 통신 단절 상황에서 **마지막으로 검증된 Evidence 또는 State를 제한된 시간 동안 제한된 목적에만 사용할 수 있도록 허용하는 정책적 시간 창**이다.

Grace Period는 무조건 허용이 아니다.

Grace Period는 통신 단절 시 임시 허용 창이다.  
Grace Period는 stale 데이터를 무제한 신뢰하는 장치가 아니다.  
Grace Period는 위험 행동을 마음대로 허용하는 예외가 아니다.

---

## **15.2 Grace Period 판단 조건**

Grace Period는 반드시 Evidence Model의 시간 신뢰, 장치 상태, 출처 신뢰 정보를 참조해야 한다.

판단 조건:

last\_verified\_state\_snapshot\_ref  
last\_verified\_evidence\_bundle\_ref

evidence\_freshness\_ttl

time\_trust\_level

device\_health\_status

trust\_upgrade\_status

action\_type

local\_edge\_policy\_available

---

추천 결과값은 다음과 같다.

GRACE\_DENIED

---

## **15.4 Grace Period 허용 원칙**

통신 단절 시 허용 가능한 것은 제한되어야 한다.

허용 가능 예시:

local dashboard warning  
safe stop  
return to safe zone  
hold task  
request manual inspection  
audit-only record

금지 또는 제한해야 하는 예시:

new high-risk approval based only on stale state  
production PLC write  
SCADA write  
robot mission expansion  
zone unlock  
permit override  
policy exception approval

---

## **15.5 Grace Period 핵심 원칙**

Stale state may support local safe action.  
Stale state must not support new risky execution.  
Communication loss must reduce authority, not expand it.

한글로 풀면 다음과 같다.

오래된 상태는 로컬 안전 행동의 근거가 될 수 있다.  
오래된 상태는 새로운 위험 실행의 근거가 될 수 없다.  
통신 단절은 권한을 늘리는 조건이 아니라 줄이는 조건이다.

---

## **15.6 Grace Period 시나리오 1: Gas Critical \+ 통신 단절**

상황:

허용하면 안 되는 행동:

핵심 판단:

통신 단절은 권한을 확장하지 않는다.  
하지만 이미 검증된 위험 증거가 있고 로컬 fail-safe 정책이 있다면,

# **16\. Emergency Policy Bypass**

Emergency Policy Bypass는 비상 상황에서 일반 승인 흐름보다 먼저 작동할 수 있는 사전 승인 정책이다.

중요한 점은 이것이다.

Emergency Policy Bypass는 무승인이 아니다.  
Emergency Policy Bypass는 사전 승인된 비상 정책이다.  
Emergency Policy Bypass는 사후 감사를 필수로 요구한다.

---

## **16.1 Emergency Policy Bypass가 필요한 이유**

비상 상황에서는 사람 승인을 기다리면 더 큰 피해가 발생할 수 있다.

예시:

gas critical threshold exceeded  
worker entered danger zone  
robot moving toward human  
fire detected  
structural collapse risk detected  
confined space emergency

이런 경우에는 사전 승인된 emergency policy에 따라 빠르게 local alarm, evacuation request, stop work, safe stop 같은 행동이 필요할 수 있다.

---

## **16.2 Emergency Policy Bypass 활성화 조건**

Emergency Policy Bypass는 다음 조건을 만족해야 한다.

emergency\_policy\_status \= ACTIVE  
risk\_level \= CRITICAL\_EMERGENCY  
minimum\_verified\_evidence\_exists \= true  
target\_entity\_is\_canonical \= true  
target\_entity\_is\_ontology\_bound \= true  
safety\_gate\_required \= true  
post\_hoc\_audit\_required \= true  
emergency\_action\_type\_allowed \= true

외부 시스템 요청이 필요한 경우 추가로 다음을 확인해야 한다.

external\_adapter\_available \= true  
network\_health\_acceptable \= true  
or local\_fail\_safe\_policy\_available \= true

---

## **16.3 Emergency Policy Bypass 금지 조건**

다음 조건에서는 Emergency Policy Bypass를 허용하면 안 된다.

LLM output only  
SLM output only  
AI\_DERIVED\_ONLY evidence only  
unverified evidence only  
unknown target  
non-canonical target  
ontology binding missing  
emergency policy inactive  
missing post-hoc audit  
production adapter unavailable without local fail-safe  
heartbeat lost without local fail-safe

---

## **16.4 Emergency Policy Bypass 권한**

Emergency Policy를 작성, 검토, 승인, 활성화할 수 있는 권한은 분리되어야 한다.

추천 권한 구조:

EmergencyPolicyAuthor  
EmergencyPolicyReviewer  
SafetyOwner  
PolicyOwner  
ExternalSystemOwner  
AuditOwner  
LegalComplianceOwner

권한 분리 원칙:

정책 작성자와 승인자는 분리한다.  
안전 정책 소유자와 외부 시스템 소유자는 분리한다.  
비상 정책 활성화는 감사 책임자와 연결한다.  
production adapter가 포함되면 ExternalSystemOwner 승인이 필요하다.

---

## **16.6 Post-Hoc Audit**

사후 감사는 다음을 검토한다.

## **30.6 Evidence Model**

정책은 증거 조건을 요구할 수 있다.

CRITICAL\_EMERGENCY requires verified operational evidence.  
ATTESTED\_AI\_DERIVED must reference trust\_upgrade\_status.

---

## **30.7 Ontology Module Boundary**

Domain module은 Core 정책을 약화시킬 수 없다.

Domain policy may extend Core policy.  
Domain policy must not weaken Core Safety Policy.  
Domain policy must not bypass Core Audit Policy.  
Domain policy must not relax Emergency Policy preconditions.

---

## **30.8 Decision / Approval Matrix**

Decision / Approval Matrix는 required approval route를 결정하고, Policy Governance Model은 그 route의 정책 조건을 검증한다.

## **30.9 Execution Adapter Model**

정책은 어떤 외부 어댑터가 어떤 조건에서 사용 가능한지 정의한다.

DryRunAdapter allowed in MVP.

## **30.10 Audit Observability Model**

정책 판단, 정책 변경, 정책 예외, 정책 충돌 해결, grace period 사용, emergency bypass 사용은 모두 감사되어야 한다.

# **34\. 최종 원칙**

Policy Governance Model은 플랫폼의 정책 헌법이다.

하지만 정책은 단독으로 실행하지 않는다.

Policy는 허용 조건을 정의한다.  
Policy Resolution은 정책 충돌을 해결한다.  
Grace Period는 통신 단절 시 권한을 제한한다.  
Emergency Policy Bypass는 사전 승인된 비상 정책이다.  
Decision은 판단 경로를 선택한다.  
Approval은 권한을 부여한다.  
Safety Gate는 실행 직전 검증한다.  
Execution Adapter는 외부 시스템에 요청한다.  
Audit은 책임을 남긴다.

정책은 canonical object 기준으로 적용되어야 한다.

정책은 Evidence Model의 source trust, time trust, clock sync, device health, trust upgrade 정보를 참조해야 한다.

정책은 버전 관리되어야 한다.

정책 충돌은 safety-first 원칙으로 해결되어야 한다.

통신 단절은 권한을 확장하는 조건이 아니라 제한하는 조건이다.

Emergency Policy Bypass는 무승인이 아니라 사전 승인된 비상 정책이다.

Emergency Policy Bypass는 반드시 사후 감사를 요구한다.

Policy Exception은 반드시 유효 기간, 범위, 보완 통제, 감사 기록을 가져야 한다.

Role은 직책이고 Clearance는 자격이다. 고위험 권한은 둘 다 필요하다.

SLM과 LLM은 ActionCandidate를 제안할 수 있지만 승인하거나 실행할 수 없다.

최종 규칙은 다음과 같다.

Policy governs permission.  
Resolution handles conflict.  
Grace Period limits authority under disconnection.  
Emergency Bypass requires pre-approved policy and post-hoc audit.  
Safety Gate protects execution.  
Audit preserves accountability.
